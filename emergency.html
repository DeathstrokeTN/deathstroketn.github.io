🚨 Emergency Guidance

Know exactly when to stop managing at home and seek immediate medical or psychiatric help.

1 Psychiatric Emergency

Go to the hospital or call emergency services immediately if the patient:

  • ❗ Threatens to harm themselves (suicidal statements or actions).
  • ❗ Threatens to physically harm you or someone else.
  • ❗ Is experiencing severe hallucinations (seeing or hearing things that aren't there and acting on them).
  • ❗ Completely stops eating or drinking for multiple days.
  • ❗ Is completely unresponsive but breathing.

2 Medical Emergency (Post-Stroke)

Call an ambulance (do not drive them yourself) if you notice:

  • ⚡ Face drooping on one side again.
  • ⚡ Arm weakness (cannot raise both arms).
  • ⚡ Speech difficulty (sudden slurring or inability to speak).
  • ⚡ Sudden, severe headache with no known cause.
  • ⚡ Sudden loss of balance or coordination, or a severe fall.

Note: Time is critical in stroke events. (Think F.A.S.T. - Face, Arms, Speech, Time).
