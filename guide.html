Caregiver Guide: What should I do?

Practical, actionable advice for difficult situations.

Interactive Problem Solver

Click on a situation below to see what to do.

😠 Patient is aggressive or angry

They are yelling, agitated, or refusing to cooperate.

  • ✅ Stay calm: Do not raise your voice. Your calmness can defuse the situation.
  • ✅ Step back: Make sure you are at a safe distance. Give them physical space.
  • ✅ Acknowledge feelings: "I see you are very upset right now."
  • ❌ Do not argue: Logic rarely works during an emotional outburst.
  • 👉 If you feel threatened, leave the room immediately and call for help.

💊 Patient refuses medication

They spit it out, hide it, or say they don't need it.

  • ✅ Find out why: Ask calmly, "Do these pills make you feel sick?" or "Are they hard to swallow?"
  • ✅ Change the timing: Try offering it when they are more relaxed, along with a favorite snack or drink.
  • ✅ Give choices: "Would you like to take your pill with water or juice?"
  • ❌ Do not force: Never force medicine into their mouth. Report consistent refusal to the doctor.

😔 Patient is completely withdrawn

They won't get out of bed, speak, or eat.

  • ✅ Just be there: Sit quietly with them. Just your presence is supportive.
  • ✅ Offer small tasks: "Can you help me fold these two towels?" Set tiny, achievable goals.
  • ✅ Ensure basic needs: Bring water and small, easy-to-eat snacks to their bedside.
  • ❌ Do not demand cheer: Don't tell them they "need to get up and do something."

🗣️ Communication Breakdown (Stroke/Aphasia)

They are trying to speak but the words don't make sense, creating frustration.

  • ✅ Use yes/no questions: "Are you hungry?" or "Does it hurt?"
  • ✅ Use visual aids: Point to objects or use a picture board.
  • ✅ Give them time: Wait patiently for their response without interrupting.
  • ❌ Do not pretend: Don't pretend you understand if you don't. Gently say, "I'm sorry, I didn't get that. Let's try again."

Managing Your Own Stress

You cannot pour from an empty cup. If you burn out, you won't be able to provide care.

The 3 Rules of Caregiver Survival:
  1. Accept that you are human. You will get frustrated, make mistakes, and feel tired. This is normal. Forgive yourself.
  2. Ask for help specifically. Don't just say "I'm overwhelmed." Tell someone: "Can you come sit with Mom for 2 hours on Thursday so I can go to the store?"
  3. Keep one hobby alive. Continue doing at least one thing for yourself every week, whether it's reading, gardening, or walking alone.
