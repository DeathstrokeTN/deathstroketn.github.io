Patient is Aggressive or Angry

High Priority Common

They are yelling, agitated, or refusing to cooperate.

🎯 Key Insight

Anger is often a sign of fear, pain, or loss of control. Address the underlying emotion, not the behavior.

  • ✅

    Stay calm: Do not raise your voice. Your calmness can defuse the situation.

  • ✅

    Step back: Make sure you are at a safe distance. Give them physical space.

  • ✅

    Acknowledge feelings: "I see you are very upset right now."

  • ❌

    Do not argue: Logic rarely works during an emotional outburst.

🚨 Emergency: If you feel unsafe, call 911 or a crisis hotline immediately.

💊

Patient Refuses Medication

Common Management

They spit it out, hide it, or say they don't need it.

💡 Pro Tip

Never force medication. Contact their healthcare provider if refusal persists beyond 24 hours.

  • ✅

    Find out why: Ask calmly, "Do these pills make you feel sick?"

  • ✅

    Change timing: Try offering it when they are more relaxed.

  • ✅

    Give choices: "Water or juice?"

  • ✅

    Disguise if needed: Check with doctor first - some meds can be crushed in food.

😔

Patient is Withdrawn

Concerning Emotional

They won't get out of bed, speak, or eat.

⚠️ Important Note

Depression is common in chronic illness. If symptoms persist >2 weeks, consult their doctor.

  • ✅

    Just be there: Sit quietly with them. Presence is supportive.

  • ✅

    Offer tiny goals: "Help me fold these two towels."

  • ✅

    Use gentle touch: A hand massage or brushing hair can be comforting.

  • ✅

    Bring in memories: Look at old photos or play favorite music.

🗣️

Communication Breakdown

Frequent Cognitive

The words don't make sense, creating frustration.

🗝️ Key Strategy

Focus on the emotion behind the words, not the exact meaning. Validate their feelings.

  • ✅

    Yes/No questions: "Are you hungry?" instead of "What do you want?"

  • ✅

    Use visual aids: Point to objects or pictures.

  • ✅

    Speak slowly and clearly: One idea at a time.

  • ✅

    Write it down: Simple written notes can bridge communication gaps.

When to Call Emergency Services Immediately

  • 🔴 Chest pain or pressure
  • 🔴 Difficulty breathing
  • 🔴 Sudden confusion or agitation
  • 🔴 Severe bleeding
  • 🔴 Loss of consciousness
  • 🔴 Signs of stroke (FAST)
  • 🔴 Seizure lasting >5 minutes
  • 🔴 Severe pain

💝 Self-Care is Essential

Managing Your Own Stress

You cannot pour from an empty cup. If you burn out, you won't be able to provide care. Your wellbeing matters.

⚠️ Warning Signs of Caregiver Burnout

  • 😴 Constant exhaustion
  • 😤 Increased irritability
  • 😔 Feeling hopeless
  • 💤 Sleep problems
  • 🏥 Getting sick more often
  • 📉 Loss of interest in activities
  • 🍷 Increased substance use
  • 😟 Anxiety about the future
The 3 Rules of Caregiver Survival:
  1. 01

    Accept that you are human. You will get frustrated and tired. This is normal. Forgive yourself. You're doing an incredibly difficult job.

  2. 02

    Ask for help specifically. Instead of "I need help," say: "Can you sit with Mom for 2 hours on Thursday so I can go to the store?" Be specific.

  3. 03

    Keep one hobby alive. Continue doing at least one thing for yourself every week—reading, gardening, calling a friend. This is non-negotiable.
